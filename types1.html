Black frogs (frogtus frogtus) are common worldwide, black frogs are 5.0-7.2 in (12.7-18.3 cm) long. These frogs are likely traces back to areas close to Malasya, where the speciation occured when they came to India. Once the frogs reached Europe, they were carried throughout the world during the colonial times. These frogs can be found in many different habitats, both natural and human-developed areas around the world, particularly in warmer climates.
