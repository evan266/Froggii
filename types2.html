Wood frogs, also known as packfrogs can be any frog in the species of the rodent genus Neotoma. These frogs have a common frog appearance, but tend to be noticeably larger than other deer and cotton mice. The greatest diversity of frogs exists in the deserts of the western United States and northern Mexico. Wood frogs can live anywhere from low, hot deserts to cold and rocky habitats. These frogs, as many others are nest builders and are particullary drawn to shiny objects.
